Academic Background

I am a Sessional Lecturer in Business Research Methods at Liverpool John Moores University (LJMU) with a PhD in Management Studies. With over 12 years of industry experience in China and £1.5 million in research grants under my belt, I blend academic rigor with practical business solutions.

My passion lies in connecting people—be it through UK-China academic collaborations or industry partnerships.

As an educator, I strive to create an engaging learning environment that combines theoretical frameworks with real-world applications. My goal is to inspire students to think critically and develop practical skills that will serve them in their future careers.

Current Position

I currently serve as a lecturer in Luxury Fashion Marketing at Liverpool John Moores University, where I teach undergraduate and postgraduate courses while conducting research in consumer behavior and digital marketing strategies.

Research

Exploring the intersection of luxury fashion, consumer behavior, and digital innovation

Consumer Behavior

Investigating the psychological and social factors that influence luxury consumption patterns and purchase decisions in both Eastern and Western markets.

Current focus: Cross-cultural differences in luxury perception and consumption motivation.

Digital Marketing

Analyzing the effectiveness of various digital marketing strategies in the luxury fashion sector, with special emphasis on social media engagement and influencer marketing.

Current focus: The impact of user-generated content on luxury brand perception.

Sustainable Fashion

Researching sustainable practices in the luxury fashion industry and analyzing consumer attitudes toward environmental responsibility in fashion consumption.

Current focus: The relationship between perceived luxury value and sustainable consumption.
